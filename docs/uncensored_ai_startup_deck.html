VoxLibre — Risks & Mitigations

Killer Risk

Coqui AI (45.5K stars) tried open-source TTS business and failed. Resemble AI controls Chatterbox and could compete directly. Thin wrapper over open-source has historically struggled to capture value.

Mitigation

Go vertical, not horizontal. Focus on audiobook production and accessibility devices. Build switching costs through voice cloning data and production tooling, not inference. ElevenLabs-compatible API for zero-friction migration.

AegisAI — Strategy

Beachhead: Cybersecurity

Security researchers cannot use frontier providers. Highest willingness to pay. Well-defined use cases (pentesting, exploit analysis, vuln review). Start with 3 design partners.

Moat: Compliance

SOC 2 Type II + FedRAMP certification creates 12-18 month barrier. Company Brain RAG pipeline creates high switching costs as it indexes institutional knowledge.

Killer Risk

Squeezed between free tools (Ollama 174K stars, vLLM) and well-funded platforms (NVIDIA NIM, Databricks). "Zero censorship" branding repels enterprise legal.

Key Positioning

NEVER say "uncensored." Say "enterprise-controlled AI" or "sovereign AI." Let CISOs set their own policies. Market the control, not the absence of restrictions.

Market: $15-30B SAM within $120-180B enterprise AI TAM

RefusalBench

The credit rating agency for AI model censorship

Why It Works

Picks & shovels play. No existing tool treats restriction behavior as a queryable dimension. 2-person team, 3-month MVP. Revenue scales with model proliferation. Serves the ecosystem instead of competing in it.

Distribution

Partner with OpenRouter (8M users, 100T tokens/mo) to integrate restriction metadata. Sell to enterprise procurement teams ($5K-25K/yr). Model providers who want transparency become allies.

Risk

Platform risk: model providers can block probing accounts. No regulatory mandate (unlike credit ratings). Gray Swan AI or Artificial Analysis could add this as a feature.

Feasibility: 9/10

Cheapest to build of all concepts. Automated probing pipeline + structured output database + web dashboard + API. Academic predecessors exist (SORRY-Bench, ICLR 2025).

Market: $2-5B (enterprise AI procurement infrastructure)

What NOT to Build

Consumer "Uncensored" Chat Platforms PASS

Venice.ai already at 12.1M visits/month growing 22.7% MoM. xAI/Grok at $120B+ valuation — this is a big-company game now. Character.AI wrongful death lawsuits preview the legal exposure. The $25-40B SAM is fantasy math (conflates free roleplay visits with paying subscribers).

Unrestricted Image Generation PASS

CivitAI (a16z-backed) is vertically integrating. FLUX.1-dev is non-commercial (Section 2.b). Payment processor risk is existential for adult content. Crowded: BasedLabs, Mage.space, RunDiffusion, SeaArt all exist.

Abliteration-as-a-Service PASS

Heretic (24K GitHub stars) delivers this for free via a single pip command. 3,500+ variants with 13M downloads. The community expects free models. No validated willingness to pay. Realistic market is $5-20M, not $1-3B.
